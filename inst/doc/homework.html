Repeat Examples 6.8 and 6.10 for Mardia multivariate skewness test. Mardia proposed tests of multivariate normality based on multivariate generalizations of skewness and kurtosis. If X and Y are iid, the multivariate population skewness \(\beta_{1,d}\) is defined by Mardia as \[\beta_{1,d}=E[(X-\mu)^T {\Sigma}^{-1}(X-\mu)]^3\] Under normality, \(\beta_{1,d}=0\). The multivariate skewness statistic is \[b_{1,d}=\frac{1}{n^2}\sum\limits_{i,j=1}^n((X_i-\bar{X})^T \widehat{\Sigma}^{-1}(X_j-\bar{X}))^3\] where \(\widehat{\Sigma}\) is the maximum likelihood estimator of covariance. Large values of \(b_{1,d}\) are significant. The asymptotic distribution of \(nb_{1,d}/6\) is chisquared with d(d + 1)(d + 2)/6 degrees of freedom.

Solution.

library(MASS)
SSig<-matrix(c(1,0,0,1),2,2)

sk=function(n){
  for(i in 1:n){
    for(j in 1:n){
      x=mvrnorm(n, rep(0,2), SSig)
      x_bar=mean(x)
      cc=cov(x)
      bb[i,j]=(t(x[i,]-x_bar)%*%solve(cc)%*%(x[j,]-x_bar))^3

  }

}

GR=function(psi){
  psi=as.matrix(psi)
  n=ncol(psi)
  k=nrow(psi)
  psi.means=rowMeans(psi) 
  B=n*var(psi.means)
  psi.w=apply(psi,1,"var")
  W=mean(psi.w) 
  v=W*(n-1)/n+(B/n)
  r=v/W

cc=function(N,X1){

  x[1]=X1

f=function(k,a){
  d=nrow(a)
  l1=(-1/2)^k/factorial(k)
  l2=(norm(a,type="F"))^(2*k+2)/(2*k+1)/(2*k+2)
  l3=exp(lgamma((d+2)/2)+lgamma(k+1.5)-lgamma(k+d/2+1))

  return(l)
}
